The Washtenaw TOPP (Transition to Organic Partnership Program) Farm Tours are a free, public event giving anyone the opportunity to visit three working sustainable farms in Washtenaw County and learn directly from the farmers who run them.

**Registration is required and closes July 17, 2026.** Register at the [MSU Extension event page](https://www.canr.msu.edu/events/topp-organic-farm-tours-explore-3-washtenaw-farms) or contact Katie Brandt at brandtk7@msu.edu or Mariel Borgman at mborgm@msu.edu. Addresses for the second and third farm stops will be sent upon registration.

## Tour Itinerary

**9:00 – 11:00 AM — Raindance Farm (2015 E North Territorial Rd, Whitmore Lake)**
Farmer Kristen Meuhlhauser leads a tour of their cut flower operation, covering ecological management practices and on-farm education. [Rain Dance Organics](/farms/rain-dance-organics) is one of the standout flower farms in the Washtenaw County area.

**11:30 AM – 2:15 PM — Nature & Nurture Seeds**
Seedsman Mike Levine hosts a tour and lunch focused on organic seed production and heirloom seed preservation.

**3:00 – 5:00 PM — Pott Farms**
Farmer Robbin Pott demonstrates regenerative organic farming practices.

## What to Know

- **Cost:** Free
- **Transportation:** Participants arrange their own transportation between the three farm stops
- **Registration deadline:** July 17, 2026
- Organized by Michigan State University Extension as part of the USDA's Transition to Organic Partnership Program
