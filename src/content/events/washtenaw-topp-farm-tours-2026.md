The Washtenaw TOPP (Transition to Organic Partnership Program) Farm Tours give the public a rare opportunity to visit working sustainable farms in Washtenaw County and learn directly from the farmers who run them.

## Tour Itinerary

**9:00 – 11:00 AM — [Rain Dance Organics](/farms/rain-dance-organics)**
Farmer Kristen Meuhlhauser leads a tour of their cut flower operation, covering ecological management practices and on-farm education programs. Rain Dance Organics is already one of the standout flower farms in the Washtenaw County area.

**11:30 AM – 2:15 PM — Nature and Nurture Farm**
Seedsman Mike Levine hosts a tour and lunch focused on organic seed production and heirloom seed preservation — a fascinating look at how seeds are saved and stewarded.

**3:00 – 5:00 PM — Pott Farms**
Farmer Robbin Pott demonstrates regenerative organic practices for hemp cultivation and covers soil health principles.

## Who It's For

This event is ideal for anyone interested in sustainable agriculture, organic farming methods, or simply curious about how the farms in your area operate. No farming background required.

## How to Register

Participants arrange their own transportation between the three farm stops. For registration and questions, contact **Katie Brandt at brandtk7@msu.edu**.

Organized by Michigan State University Extension as part of the USDA's Transition to Organic Partnership Program.
