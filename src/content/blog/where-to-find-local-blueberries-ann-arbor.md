Strawberry season is wrapping up, but something even better is right behind it. Michigan blueberries typically start showing up at local farmers markets in early-to-mid July, and they run through August. Michigan consistently ranks among the top blueberry-producing states in the country — the sandy, acidic soil in many parts of the state is ideal growing conditions — and local blueberries are noticeably better than anything shipped from out of state.

Here's what you need to know.

## What Makes Michigan Blueberries Different

Local blueberries are picked ripe. Commercially grown berries shipped across the country are often harvested before they're fully ripe so they survive the journey. A blueberry picked at peak ripeness and eaten within a day or two is sweeter, more flavorful, and a completely different experience.

They're also more nutritious — blueberries are highest in antioxidants at peak ripeness, which is exactly when local farmers pick them.

## When to Expect Them

In Washtenaw County, local blueberries typically appear at farmers markets between early and mid-July, depending on the weather that season. The window runs through mid-to-late August. Check with vendors at the markets in early July — they'll tell you exactly when their crop is ready.

## Where to Find Them

**At the farmers markets:** All four [Washtenaw County farmers markets](/markets) — Ann Arbor, Chelsea, Saline, and Ypsilanti — will have blueberry vendors when the season arrives. The Ann Arbor Farmers Market typically has the widest selection. Arrive early on Saturdays — blueberries go fast.

**Farm stands and u-pick:** [Kapnick Orchards](/farms/kapnick-orchards) in Britton and [Wasem Fruit Farm](/farms/wasem-fruit-farm) in Milan both grow fruit crops and are good options for picking up larger quantities. Check their websites or call ahead to confirm blueberry availability and u-pick options before making the drive.

## How Much to Buy

If you find good local blueberries, buy more than you think you need. They freeze exceptionally well — better than almost any other fruit. Spread them in a single layer on a sheet pan, freeze until solid (a few hours), then transfer to a zip bag. Frozen local blueberries are excellent in smoothies, pancakes, muffins, and sauces all winter long.

## What to Make

Fresh blueberries need almost no preparation. A handful over yogurt, in oatmeal, or eaten straight out of the container is perfect. When you want to cook with them:

- **Blueberry lemon muffins** — check our [recipe here](/recipes/blueberry-lemon-muffins)
- **Blueberry pancakes** — fold them in frozen so they don't bleed into the batter
- **Simple blueberry sauce** — simmer with a little sugar and lemon juice for 10 minutes; pour over ice cream, yogurt, or waffles
- **Blueberry crisp** — same technique as the [strawberry rhubarb crisp](/recipes/strawberry-rhubarb-crisp), just swap the fruit

## How to Store Them

Don't wash until right before eating. Store unwashed in the refrigerator in a shallow container. They keep well for 5–7 days — longer than strawberries. For best flavor, let them come to room temperature for 15–20 minutes before eating.

July is peak summer at the Washtenaw County farmers markets. Blueberries are just one of many reasons to go — corn, zucchini, green beans, cucumbers, and the first tomatoes are all arriving at the same time. It's one of the best months of the year to shop local.
