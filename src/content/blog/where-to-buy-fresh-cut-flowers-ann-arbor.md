Mid-July is peak flower season in Washtenaw County — zinnias, dahlias, sunflowers, and snapdragons are all hitting their stride at once. If you've only ever bought flowers from a grocery store cooler, a local bouquet is a different experience entirely: they're cut days (not weeks) before you get them, they actually smell like something, and the money stays with a farm down the road instead of a shipping container.

## Dedicated Flower Farms

A few Washtenaw County farms grow flowers as their main crop, not a side project. [Backbeat Flower Farm](/farms/backbeat-flower-farm) near Chelsea runs a roadside farm stand Fridays and Saturdays, May through October, with fresh-cut bouquets and seasonal arrangements grown on a small family farm. [Marilla Field & Flora](/farms/marilla-field-and-flora) in Dexter offers flower CSA subscriptions and custom floral design, including a full-season share of weekly bouquets if you want fresh flowers on a schedule without thinking about it. And [Rain Dance Organics](/farms/rain-dance-organics), a certified organic flower farm on 44 acres in Whitmore Lake, sells bouquet subscriptions, bulk "Buckets of Blooms" for events, and runs u-pick flower events from July through September.

## U-Pick Flowers

If you'd rather cut your own, [Rain Dance Organics](/farms/rain-dance-organics) hosts u-pick flower events all summer, and [Jacob's Fresh Farm](/farms/jacobs-fresh-farm) in Dexter offers u-pick sunflowers and lavender in addition to their u-pick strawberries. Both are good options for a low-key summer outing — bring a bucket, cut what you like, and you'll walk away with a bouquet no shop arrangement can match.

## Vegetable Farms That Also Grow Flowers

Flowers aren't just for the specialty growers. Several of the area's vegetable CSA farms grow cut flowers alongside their produce, often as an add-on share or a few bunches at the farm stand: [Blue Spring Farm](/farms/blue-spring-farm), [Fusilier Family Farms](/farms/fusilier-family-farms), [Green Things Farm Collective](/farms/green-things-farm), [Seva Farm](/farms/seva-farm), [Slow Farm Organic](/farms/slow-farm-organic), [Robin Hills Farm](/farms/robin-hills-farm), and [Tantre Farm](/farms/tantre-farm) all grow flowers in the mix. If you're already picking up a CSA share from one of these farms, ask about adding a flower share — it's often one of the easiest upgrades available.

## At the Farmers Markets

All four [Washtenaw County farmers markets](/markets) — Ann Arbor, Chelsea, Saline, and Ypsilanti — have vendors selling bouquets by the bunch in peak season. Go early on a Saturday if you want first pick of the dahlias; they move fast.

## A Few Tips for Making Them Last

Locally grown flowers aren't bred for a two-week shelf life the way shipped flowers are, but a few habits go a long way. Recut stems at an angle before putting them in water, change the water every couple of days, and keep the vase out of direct sun and away from a fruit bowl — ripening fruit releases ethylene gas that shortens a bouquet's life. Strip any leaves that would sit below the waterline; they rot fast and cloud the water.

Whether you want a standing weekly subscription or just a single bunch for the kitchen table, Washtenaw County has more local flower growers than most people realize. Browse the full [farms](/farms) directory to find one near you.
