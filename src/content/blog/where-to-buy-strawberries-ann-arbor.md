Michigan strawberry season is one of the most fleeting things in local food. It lasts maybe two to three weeks — usually the second half of June — and then it's over until next year. The strawberries you find at local farms and farmers markets during that window are nothing like what you get from a grocery store. They're smaller, softer, deeper red, and intensely sweet. Worth planning around.

Here's where to find them.

## Farmers Markets

The fastest way to get local strawberries is to show up early at one of the [Washtenaw County farmers markets](/markets) on a Saturday morning. Ann Arbor, Saline, Chelsea, and Dexter all run weekly markets through the summer.

**A few tips for market strawberry shopping:**
- Arrive at or before opening time. Strawberries from local farms sell out fast — often by 9 or 10am on Saturdays.
- Bring cash. It's faster and always works.
- Buy more than you think you need. They don't last long, but they freeze beautifully — hull them, spread on a sheet pan to freeze solid, then transfer to a bag.

## Farm Stands and CSAs

Several Washtenaw County farms grow strawberries as part of their CSA shares or sell them directly.

**[Tantre Farm](/farms/tantre-farm)** in Chelsea is one of the most established organic farms in the county and typically has strawberries at both the Ann Arbor and Chelsea markets. They're a reliable source and often one of the first to have them.

**[Community Farm of Ann Arbor](/farms/community-farm-of-ann-arbor)** in Webster Township is a CSA farm with hundreds of members. If you're in their CSA, strawberries are one of the early-summer highlights. They also sell at the Ann Arbor Farmers Market.

**[Kapnick Orchards](/farms/kapnick-orchards)** in Britton runs a u-pick operation that includes strawberries when in season. Worth checking their website or calling ahead to confirm availability before making the drive.

**[Wasem Fruit Farm](/farms/wasem-fruit-farm)** in Milan has been growing fruit in Washtenaw County for over a century and typically offers strawberries for pick-your-own and pre-picked during the season.

## How to Store Them

Local strawberries are more perishable than grocery store berries, which are bred for shelf life. To get the most out of them:

- Don't wash until right before eating — moisture accelerates mold
- Store in the refrigerator in a single layer if possible, or loosely in a container with airflow
- Plan to use within 1–2 days
- **Freeze extras:** Hull them, freeze flat on a sheet pan, then store in a zip bag. They're great in smoothies, baked goods, and sauces all year

## What to Make

The simplest things are often the best: strawberries with a little sugar and cream, sliced over yogurt, or in a salad with spinach and a honey balsamic dressing. Check our [strawberry spinach salad recipe](/recipes/strawberry-spinach-salad) for a quick seasonal idea.

Don't wait. The window is short — and once it's closed, you're waiting until June 2027.
