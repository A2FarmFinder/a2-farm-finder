If you grew up in Michigan, you know the feeling: the first bite of truly fresh sweet corn, picked that morning, is a completely different food than anything from a grocery store bin. The good news is that season is right around the corner for Washtenaw County.

## When to Expect It

Sweet corn is a late-summer crop. In this part of Michigan, it typically starts showing up at farm stands and farmers markets in late July, hits its stride through August, and often runs into early September depending on the weather. Early varieties come first in smaller quantities; by mid-August most local growers have it in full supply.

## Where to Buy It

**At the farmers markets:** All four [Washtenaw County farmers markets](/markets) — Ann Arbor, Chelsea, Saline, and Ypsilanti — will have corn vendors once the season kicks in. It sells fast on weekend mornings, so don't wait until noon.

**At the vegetable farms and CSAs:** Most of the area's diversified vegetable growers plant sweet corn as part of their summer rotation. Keep an eye on [Tantre Farm](/farms/tantre-farm), [Community Farm of Ann Arbor](/farms/community-farm-of-ann-arbor), [Jacob's Fresh Farm](/farms/jacobs-fresh-farm), [Seva Farms](/farms/seva-farm), [Green Things Farm Collective](/farms/green-things-farm), [Old City Acres](/farms/old-city-acres), [Slow Farm Organic](/farms/slow-farm-organic), [Fusilier Family Farms](/farms/fusilier-family-farms), [Blue Spring Farm](/farms/blue-spring-farm), [Arbor Hills Farm](/farms/arbor-hills-farm), [Robin Hills Farm](/farms/robin-hills-farm), and [Hand Sown Farm](/farms/hand-sown-farm) — any of these CSA and farm-stand growers may have corn in their late-summer share or stand once it's ready. If you're a CSA member, it's worth asking your farm directly when corn is expected in your box.

**At year-round farm markets:** [Kapnick Orchards](/farms/kapnick-orchards) in Britton runs a year-round farm market that carries seasonal fruits and vegetables alongside their bakery and cider, and is worth a stop if you're already out that way.

**At Argus Farm Stop:** [Argus Farm Stop](/farms/argus-farm-stop) works with 200+ Michigan farms and restocks daily, so it's a reliable bet for local corn even if you can't make it to a Saturday market.

## Make It a Full Summer Cookout Run

Sweet corn practically demands a cookout, so consider making a trip of it. Pick up grass-fed beef for burgers from [Baseline Farm](/farms/baseline-farm), or pastured chicken and lamb from [Whitney Farmstead](/farms/whitney-farmstead), and you've got the whole meal covered from local sources. If you're headed out toward Milan anyway, swing by [Wasem Fruit Farm](/farms/wasem-fruit-farm) — raspberry season overlaps with early corn, and their donuts don't hurt either.

## How to Pick Good Corn

Look for bright green, tightly wrapped husks and golden-brown (not black or dry) silk poking out the top. Give an ear a gentle squeeze — it should feel plump and full all the way to the tip. Skip anything with husks that look yellowed or dried out; that's a sign it's been sitting a while.

## How to Store It

Corn's sugars start converting to starch the moment it's picked, so the flavor is best the same day. If you can't cook it right away, keep it in the husk, refrigerated, and use it within a day or two.
