Beets and cucumbers hit the market at the same time in July, and this chilled soup puts them together in a bowl that's as refreshing as it is beautiful. Roasting the beets first brings out their sweetness, and a swirl of yogurt and a shower of fresh dill balance the earthy color with a bright, cooling finish. It's a great make-ahead lunch that only gets better as it chills.

## Ingredients

- 1½ lbs beets (about 4 medium), trimmed
- 1 large cucumber, peeled and roughly chopped, plus extra diced for garnish
- 2 cups plain yogurt or buttermilk, plus more for swirling
- 1 cup cold water or vegetable broth
- 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
- 1 small garlic clove
- 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped, plus more for garnish
- 2 scallions, thinly sliced, plus more for garnish
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Olive oil, for drizzling

## Instructions

1. **Roast the beets.** Preheat the oven to 400°F. Wrap the beets in foil and roast for 45–60 minutes, until a knife slides in easily. Let cool, then slip off the skins and roughly chop.

2. **Blend the soup.** Add the roasted beets, cucumber, yogurt, water or broth, vinegar, garlic, dill, and half the scallions to a blender. Blend until completely smooth.

3. **Season.** Add the salt and a few grinds of pepper, then blend again briefly. Taste and adjust with more vinegar or salt — it should taste bright, not flat.

4. **Chill.** Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, and up to 3 days. The color deepens and the flavor rounds out as it sits.

5. **Serve.** Ladle into bowls, swirl in a spoonful of extra yogurt, and top with diced cucumber, the remaining scallions, fresh dill, a drizzle of olive oil, and a crack of black pepper.

## Tips

- **No oven needed in a heat wave.** Look for pre-roasted beets at the farmers market — several vendors sell them ready to go.
- **Make it dairy-free.** Swap the yogurt for a full-fat coconut yogurt or simply use more water or broth and add extra olive oil for richness.
- **Golden beets work too.** They give a milder flavor and a completely different, sunny color if you want a change from the classic deep red.
- **Turn it into a meal.** Top with a soft-boiled egg or some cooked, chilled shrimp for a more filling lunch.
