July is when corn and tomatoes finally overlap at the market, and this salsa is the easiest way to lock in that combination before the season moves on. It skips the water-bath canner entirely — just simmer everything down, cool, and freeze in portions. Come January, a container of this thawed out next to a bag of chips will taste like the middle of summer.

## Ingredients

- 4 cups fresh corn kernels (about 6 ears)
- 3 lbs ripe tomatoes, cored and diced
- 1 cup diced onion
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- ½ cup fresh basil, chopped
- 2 tablespoons lime juice
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin
- Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)

## Instructions

1. **Char the corn.** Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the corn kernels and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned in spots, about 5–7 minutes.

2. **Build the salsa base.** Add the onion and garlic to the pot with the corn and cook 3–4 minutes, until the onion softens.

3. **Add the tomatoes.** Stir in the tomatoes, salt, cumin, and red pepper flakes if using. Bring to a simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, for 15–20 minutes, until the mixture thickens slightly and the tomatoes break down.

4. **Finish.** Remove from the heat and stir in the lime juice and fresh basil. Taste and adjust salt or lime as needed.

5. **Cool and portion.** Let the salsa cool completely, then divide into freezer-safe containers or zip-top bags, leaving ½ inch of headspace. Freeze for up to 6 months; thaw overnight in the fridge before serving.

## Tips

- **Fresh, not frozen, for the best texture.** Freshly cut corn keeps a nice bite even after freezing — it holds up better than kernels that were already frozen once.
- **Chunky or smooth, your call.** Pulse a portion in a food processor before freezing if you prefer a smoother, dip-like salsa.
- **Roast the tomatoes for deeper flavor.** Halving and roasting the tomatoes at 425°F for 20 minutes before adding them concentrates their sweetness even further.
- **Straight from frozen.** This salsa also works simmered straight from frozen into a quick topping for eggs, tacos, or grilled chicken.
