July zucchini from the farmers market is a different animal than the waxy, oversized ones at the grocery store. Local zucchini is tender, sweet, and hits the grill beautifully — and this recipe lets it shine. Feta and fresh herbs are all it needs.

## Ingredients

- 3–4 medium zucchini (about 2 lbs)
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- ½ teaspoon kosher salt
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- 3 oz crumbled feta cheese
- ¼ cup fresh basil leaves, torn
- 2 tablespoons fresh herbs (parsley, mint, or chives — or a mix)
- 1 lemon, halved
- Red pepper flakes (optional)

## Instructions

1. **Prep the zucchini.** Trim the ends and slice lengthwise into planks about ⅓ inch thick. Too thin and they fall apart; too thick and they don't cook through.

2. **Season.** Brush both sides generously with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Let sit for 5 minutes while the grill heats up.

3. **Grill.** Heat grill to medium-high (about 400°F). Grill zucchini planks 3–4 minutes per side, until grill marks form and the flesh is tender but not mushy. Don't move them around — let them sit and get a good char.

4. **Arrange.** Transfer to a serving platter in a single layer. Immediately squeeze half the lemon over the top while still hot.

5. **Top and serve.** Scatter crumbled feta, torn basil, and fresh herbs over everything. Add a pinch of red pepper flakes if you like heat. Serve warm or at room temperature.

## Tips

- **Buy small to medium zucchini** — larger ones have more seeds and less flavor. At the market, the smaller ones go fast.
- **Don't skip the resting step.** Salting the zucchini and letting it sit draws out a little moisture and improves the texture on the grill.
- **Room temperature is fine.** This dish holds well and is just as good after it cools slightly, making it easy to prep ahead for a cookout.
- **Add tomatoes.** If early tomatoes are at the market, a few halved cherry tomatoes tossed on for the last minute of grilling are great alongside.

## Where to Find the Ingredients

Zucchini and fresh herbs are abundant at Washtenaw County farmers markets all through July and August. Look for mixed herb bunches — most vendors sell them for a couple dollars and they'll last the week. Farmers markets to check: [Ann Arbor Farmers Market](/markets/ann-arbor-farmers-market), [Saline Farmers Market](/markets/saline-farmers-market), [Chelsea Farmers Market](/markets/chelsea-farmers-market).
