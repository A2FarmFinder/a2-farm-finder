By mid-July, zucchini and sweet corn are both arriving at the farmers market in force. Fritters are one of the best ways to use them — quick to make, crispy on the outside, tender inside, and good enough to eat standing over the pan. Serve them as a side dish, a snack, or top them with a fried egg for a simple summer dinner.

## Ingredients

- 2 medium zucchini (about 1 lb total), grated
- 1½ cups fresh sweet corn kernels (from about 2 ears) or frozen corn, thawed
- 3 scallions, thinly sliced
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
- ¼ cup grated Parmesan
- 2 tablespoons fresh herbs, chopped (parsley, chives, or basil)
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- ¼ teaspoon garlic powder
- Olive oil or vegetable oil for frying

**For serving:**
- Sour cream or plain Greek yogurt
- Hot sauce (optional)
- Extra fresh herbs

## Instructions

1. Grate the zucchini on the large holes of a box grater. Place in a clean kitchen towel or several layers of paper towels and squeeze out as much water as possible. This step is critical — wet zucchini makes soggy fritters.

2. In a large bowl, combine the squeezed zucchini, corn, scallions, eggs, flour, Parmesan, herbs, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Stir until just combined. The mixture should hold together when pressed — if it's too wet, add a tablespoon more flour.

3. Heat 2–3 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.

4. Scoop about 3 tablespoons of batter per fritter into the pan, pressing gently to flatten to about ½ inch thick. Cook in batches — don't crowd the pan or they'll steam instead of crisp.

5. Cook 3–4 minutes per side until deep golden brown. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Add more oil between batches as needed.

6. Serve hot with sour cream or yogurt on the side.

## Tips

- **Squeeze the zucchini well.** This is the most important step. Zucchini holds a surprising amount of water — if you skip this, the fritters will be soft and won't crisp up properly.
- **Cut the corn fresh.** Stand an ear of corn upright in a bowl and cut downward with a sharp knife. The bowl catches the kernels as they fall.
- **Keep them warm.** Place finished fritters on a baking sheet in a 200°F oven while you cook the rest.
- **Make them gluten-free.** Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend or almond flour. Both work well here.

## Where to find the ingredients

Zucchini typically starts appearing at Washtenaw County farmers markets in early-to-mid July and is one of the most abundant crops through August. Sweet corn usually arrives by mid-July. Look for both from [Tantre Farm](/farms/tantre-farm), [Community Farm of Ann Arbor](/farms/community-farm-of-ann-arbor), [Jacob's Fresh Farm](/farms/jacobs-fresh-farm), [Green Things Farm](/farms/green-things-farm), [Fusilier Family Farms](/farms/fusilier-family-farms), and [Old City Acres](/farms/old-city-acres). Fresh herbs and scallions are available throughout the summer at all four [Washtenaw County markets](/markets).
