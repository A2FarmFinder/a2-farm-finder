Early tomatoes are just starting to show up at Washtenaw County markets, and this galette is the best way to let them be the star. A galette is really just a pie that doesn't need to be perfect — the edges are folded over rustically, no crimping required. Spread over a layer of whipped ricotta and topped with fresh basil, it turns simple ingredients into a dinner that feels like a special occasion.

## Ingredients

**For the crust:**
- 1¼ cups all-purpose flour
- ½ teaspoon kosher salt
- ½ cup (1 stick) cold unsalted butter, cubed
- 3–4 tablespoons ice water

**For the filling:**
- 1½ cups whole-milk ricotta
- ¼ cup grated Parmesan
- 1 small garlic clove, grated
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1½ lbs mixed tomatoes, sliced ¼-inch thick
- ½ teaspoon kosher salt (for the tomatoes)
- 1 tablespoon cornmeal or breadcrumbs (optional, keeps the crust from getting soggy)
- ¼ cup fresh basil, torn, plus more for serving
- 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
- Olive oil for drizzling

## Instructions

1. **Make the crust.** Combine flour and salt in a bowl. Cut in the cold butter with a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture looks like coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces of butter remaining. Add ice water one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together. Form into a disk, wrap, and refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

2. **Salt the tomatoes.** While the dough chills, lay the sliced tomatoes on a paper-towel-lined tray, sprinkle with ½ teaspoon salt, and let sit for 15–20 minutes. Pat dry before using — this step keeps the galette from turning watery.

3. **Make the whipped ricotta.** Combine ricotta, Parmesan, and garlic in a bowl. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.

4. **Roll out the dough.** On a lightly floured surface, roll the chilled dough into a rough 12-inch circle. Transfer to a parchment-lined baking sheet.

5. **Assemble.** Sprinkle the cornmeal or breadcrumbs over the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border. Spread the whipped ricotta mixture over the cornmeal. Arrange the tomato slices on top, overlapping slightly, and scatter with torn basil.

6. **Fold and bake.** Fold the border of the dough up and over the edge of the filling, pleating as you go. Brush the crust with beaten egg. Bake at 400°F for 40–45 minutes, until the crust is deep golden brown.

7. **Finish and serve.** Let the galette cool for 10 minutes before slicing. Drizzle with olive oil and scatter fresh basil over the top just before serving.

## Variations

- **Add corn.** Fresh sweet corn kernels tucked in with the tomatoes add a nice pop of sweetness.
- **Cheese swap.** Goat cheese or a mix of mozzarella and ricotta both work well in place of straight ricotta.
- **Make it ahead.** The dough can be made up to 2 days in advance, or frozen for up to a month. The assembled, unbaked galette also freezes well — bake straight from frozen, adding about 10 extra minutes.
- **Serve it as a side.** Cut into smaller wedges and serve alongside grilled chicken or fish instead of as the main event.
