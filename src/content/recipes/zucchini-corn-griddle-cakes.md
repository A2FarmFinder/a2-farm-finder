If your CSA box or garden is drowning you in zucchini right now, this is a good way to eat through a few at once. Grated zucchini and fresh corn kernels get folded into a simple batter and griddled until the edges turn golden and crisp. Top with a fried egg and you've got a full breakfast; serve them plain with sour cream and they're a great light lunch too.

## Ingredients

- 2 medium zucchini, grated (about 2½ cups)
- ½ teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
- 2 ears fresh corn, kernels cut off (about 1½ cups)
- 3 scallions, thinly sliced
- 2 large eggs
- ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
- ¼ cup grated Parmesan
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- Butter or oil, for the griddle
- Sour cream, for serving

## Instructions

1. **Salt and drain the zucchini.** Toss the grated zucchini with ½ teaspoon salt in a colander set over a bowl. Let sit for 10 minutes, then squeeze firmly in a clean kitchen towel to remove as much liquid as possible — this keeps the cakes from turning soggy.

2. **Mix the batter.** In a large bowl, combine the drained zucchini, corn kernels, and scallions. Add the eggs and stir to coat. Fold in the flour, Parmesan, and pepper until just combined.

3. **Heat the griddle.** Warm a large skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a thin layer of butter or oil.

4. **Cook the cakes.** Drop the batter by ¼-cup scoops onto the griddle, flattening slightly with the back of a spoon. Cook 3–4 minutes per side, until deep golden brown and cooked through. Work in batches, adding more butter as needed.

5. **Serve warm.** Top with a dollop of sour cream, extra scallions, or a fried egg.

## Tips

- **Don't skip the squeeze.** Zucchini releases a surprising amount of water — skipping this step is the most common reason griddle cakes fall apart or steam instead of crisping.
- **Cut the corn raw.** No need to cook it first; it cooks through in the few minutes on the griddle and keeps its sweet crunch.
- **Make them ahead.** Cooked cakes reheat well in a dry skillet or toaster oven — they lose their crisp edge in the microwave.
- **Swap the cheese.** Feta or a sharp cheddar both work in place of Parmesan for a different flavor direction.
