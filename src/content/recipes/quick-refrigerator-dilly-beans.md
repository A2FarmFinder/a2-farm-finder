Green beans come on fast and heavy in July, and pickling is the easiest way to keep up with a big market haul. These dilly beans skip the canning pot entirely — just pack jars, pour a hot brine over, and let the fridge do the rest. They're ready to snack on within a day and hold their crunch for weeks, which makes them a great make-ahead addition to a picnic spread or a Bloody Mary garnish.

## Ingredients

- 1½ lbs fresh green beans, trimmed to fit your jars
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 4 sprigs fresh dill (or 2 teaspoons dill seed)
- 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
- ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
- 1½ cups white vinegar
- 1½ cups water
- 3 tablespoons kosher salt
- 1 tablespoon sugar

## Instructions

1. **Prep the jars.** Wash two quart-size jars (or several smaller ones) with hot soapy water. Divide the garlic, dill, peppercorns, and red pepper flakes evenly between the jars.

2. **Pack the beans.** Trim the green beans so they stand upright with about ½ inch of headspace below the jar rim. Pack them in tightly, standing up.

3. **Make the brine.** In a small saucepan, combine the vinegar, water, salt, and sugar. Bring to a boil, stirring until the salt and sugar dissolve.

4. **Pour and cool.** Carefully pour the hot brine over the beans, covering them completely and leaving about ½ inch of headspace. Let the jars cool uncovered on the counter for 30 minutes.

5. **Chill.** Seal the jars and refrigerate for at least 24 hours before eating — 3 days gives the best flavor. Keeps in the fridge for up to 2 months.

## Tips

- **Choose beans that snap, not bend.** Fresh-picked market beans give the crispest pickle. Limp beans stay limp.
- **Mix it up.** Wax beans or a mix of green and yellow both pickle beautifully — the color combination looks great in the jar.
- **Adjust the heat.** Add a whole small dried chile or a sliced jalapeño to each jar for a spicier version.
- **Beyond snacking.** Chop and stir into potato salad or egg salad, or use as a crunchy, tangy garnish for a Bloody Mary.
