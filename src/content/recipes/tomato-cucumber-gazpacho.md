There's nothing better on a sticky July evening than a soup that never sees a stove. This gazpacho leans on the first wave of juicy market tomatoes and cool, crisp cucumbers, blended smooth with basil, garlic, and a good glug of olive oil. It gets better as it sits, so it's an easy make-ahead lunch all week long.

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs ripe tomatoes, cored and roughly chopped
- 1 large cucumber, peeled and roughly chopped
- ½ cup diced onion (any variety on hand)
- 1 small garlic clove
- ¼ cup fresh basil leaves, plus more for serving
- 3 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for drizzling
- 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar or sherry vinegar
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
- Freshly ground black pepper

## Instructions

1. **Combine the base.** Add the tomatoes, cucumber, onion, garlic, and basil to a blender or food processor.

2. **Blend.** Pulse until mostly smooth, then add the olive oil, vinegar, and salt. Blend until fully smooth, or leave it slightly chunky if you prefer more texture.

3. **Taste and adjust.** Gazpacho needs more salt than you'd expect since it's served cold — taste and add more salt, vinegar, or pepper until it tastes bright and balanced.

4. **Chill.** Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, and up to 2 days. The flavor improves the longer it sits.

5. **Serve.** Ladle into bowls, drizzle with olive oil, and scatter with torn basil leaves and a crack of black pepper.

## Tips

- **Peel the tomatoes if you want it silkier.** Score an X in the bottom, drop in boiling water for 30 seconds, then into an ice bath — the skins slip right off.
- **Add crunch.** A small diced cucumber or bell pepper stirred in at the end (instead of blended) adds nice texture against the smooth base.
- **Make it a meal.** Top with a scoop of good tuna, a soft-boiled egg, or torn croutons for something more filling.
- **No blender needed for a rustic version.** Finely dice everything by hand for a chunky, salsa-like take instead of a smooth soup.
